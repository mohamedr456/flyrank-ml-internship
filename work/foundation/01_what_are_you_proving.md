# What Are You Proving?

*FlyRank AI Fluency — Week 1 · Mohamed Rabea*

## Proof statement

I can take messy, real-world search data and turn it into a validated machine-learning
model whose output is a ranked action list a non-technical person can actually execute —
and I can show my work. The one skill I'm proving: **end-to-end ML judgment on real data**
— framing the question, defining labels without leakage, validating honestly, and
translating scores into decisions. The one person this is for: a hiring manager filling a
junior ML / data science seat at a company that lives on production data (think: an
analytics or growth lead at an e-commerce or content company). The one action I want them
to take: **read my deployed capstone paper, then email me to talk about the role.**

## Why this needs to exist

My CV and LinkedIn can say "Python, SQL, scikit-learn" — so can ten thousand others. What
they cannot prove is judgment: that I define a success metric before touching a model
(Precision@50 under a client-holdout split, in my current capstone), that I check for
leakage before celebrating a score, and that I write results in honest, decision-support
language instead of hype. Owning a public page with the shipped work fixes exactly that:
the claim stops being a line on a CV and becomes something a stranger can verify in five
minutes.

## Self-check against the bar

- The claim is narrow and named: end-to-end ML judgment on real search data — not "I'm
  passionate about AI."
- The audience is one specific person: the hiring manager for a junior ML/data role, not
  "everyone."
- One action: read the paper, then email me.
- This statement could only describe my proof: it names my metric, my validation design,
  and my capstone. It doesn't fit a generic portfolio.
