> **Prompt:** "Here is my sitemap: Landing (claim + number вҶ’ read the paper), Work

Run for real in the Project on 2026-07-15 (chat: "Optimizing portfolio sitemap for
hiring manager conversion"; screenshot attached to the submission).

> **Output (excerpts, verbatim):** "вҖҰthe map has a contradiction: you've named the
> action as 'read the capstone paper, then email me,' and then built four pages where
> three of them end at 'read the paper' and one ends at 'email.' That's two competing
> endpoints, and the reader hits the fork before they hit the paper. вҖҰ Landing вҶ’ Work is
> a wasted hop. A hiring manager gives you maybe 30 seconds. вҖҰ A separate Contact page
> with email/GitHub/LinkedIn is a directory, not a call to action вҖ” and directories leak
> attention. Merge Contact into About (or into the footer of every page) and kill it as
> a standalone page. вҖҰ The paper itself is your biggest unexamined risk. вҖҰ The paper
> needs its own CTA вҖ” 'Questions? Email me' вҖ” because that's the actual moment of
> decision, and it happens off your site. What I'd ship: **one page.**"

**What I'll change (noted, as the bar requires):** it was blunt and it was right about
the leaks вҖ” three changes adopted: (1) **Contact stops being a page** вҖ” it folds into
About plus a footer on every page; (2) the **capstone abstract gets inlined** on the
Work section with the link going to the full paper; (3) the **paper itself ends with
"Questions? Email me"** so the action lives where the decision happens. Where I'm
pushing back (my call, stated): I'm keeping Work and About as pages rather than shipping
literally one page вҖ” the case-study depth *is* the proof for a skeptical manager вҖ” but
